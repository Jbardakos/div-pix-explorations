Full System Narrative

THE COMPLETE ARC

a body enters · is scanned · encoded · fragmented · sold · returns as residue

01
Phase I · Silence
ENTRY
The visitor enters the space. There is no announcement. The system is already watching. Ambient conditions register before conscious engagement begins.
spacepresencelatency
02
Phase II · Encounter
OBJECT
A sculpture is present. Its surface appears organic — biological, almost familiar. It is a body that has already been processed. This is not obvious yet.
formmaterialambiguity
03
Phase III · Signal
ACTIVATION
Proximity crosses a threshold. An orb of light responds. The system acknowledges the visitor's presence — not as person but as data-generating body in space.
thresholdtriggersignal
04
Phase IV · Scan
CAPTURE
A camera isolates the face. Bounding boxes appear. Features are extracted. The body becomes a collection of measurable attributes — distance, proportion, expression class.
detectionbiometricmesh
05
Phase V · Latent
ENCODING
The extracted features are passed through a machine learning model. The face collapses into a vector — a string of floating point numbers that is simultaneously the person and not the person.
embeddingvectorabstraction
06
Phase VI · Dissolution
TRANSFORM
The encoded data drives a real-time deformation of the sculpture. The biological form begins to decay — not from time but from the logic of the system. Geometry degrades. What remains is structure without surface.
decaydeformskeleton
07
Phase VII · Market
CIRCULATION
Fragments of the encoded body are released into a simulated data marketplace. They are packaged, priced, recombined. The logic of surveillance capitalism made visible and spatial.
commodityfragmentflow
08
Phase VIII · Output
RETURN
The sculpture that greeted the visitor on entry is now revealed for what it is — the skeletal residue of a previous body, processed, extracted, circulated, and returned as art object. The cycle is complete.
residueoutputreturn

Process Note · Capture
The camera isolates a face at approximately 1.5–2m distance. A bounding box appears in the interface — not to frame the face aesthetically but to define the region of interest for feature extraction. The visitor sees themselves framed as data.
